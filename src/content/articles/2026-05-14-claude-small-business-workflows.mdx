## TL;DR

Anthropic's small-business launch is useful because it moves AI from a blank chat box into actual business systems: accounting, payments, CRM, documents, design, email, and calendar. The main idea is not more prompting. It is choosing a real workflow and keeping approval before anything sends or pays.

## What's New

Claude for Small Business is a package of connectors and ready-to-run workflows inside Claude Cowork. Anthropic lists integrations with QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, DocuSign, Google Workspace, and Microsoft 365.

The launch includes workflows for tasks such as payroll planning, monthly close, invoice follow-up, sales campaigns, contract review, lead triage, and business dashboards. Anthropic says the owner starts the task and approves key actions before external changes happen.

Anthropic is also pairing the product launch with training. The AI Fluency for Small Business course is meant to teach owners which tasks are good fits for AI and how to use the tools safely.

## Why It Matters

Small businesses rarely have time to build custom automations. They do have repeated administrative work: reconciling payments, chasing invoices, drafting campaigns, answering customers, and preparing accountant-ready summaries.

This is the shape AI tools are likely to take for many people: not a standalone chatbot, but an assistant that can read across the stack, prepare work, and ask for approval at the boundary.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="25 min" prerequisites={["One recurring business task", "Access to the business tool that holds the data"]}>

1. Pick one painful recurring task, such as invoice reminders, weekly sales follow-up, month-end bookkeeping, or campaign planning.
2. Write the systems involved. Example: QuickBooks for balances, PayPal for payments, HubSpot for customers, Google Drive for documents.
3. Write the approval boundary before using any agent: "Draft only," "Prepare but do not send," or "Ask me before changing records."
4. Run the task on a small slice, such as five invoices or one campaign, and compare the output against what you would normally do manually.
5. Save the successful prompt and checklist as your first reusable business workflow.

</PracticalGuide>
