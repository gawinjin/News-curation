## TL;DR

OpenAI is previewing a finance experience in ChatGPT that can connect to financial accounts, summarize spending, and answer questions in context. Treat it like a planning assistant, not a financial adviser. Start with review-only questions before connecting anything sensitive.

## What's New

The preview is rolling out to ChatGPT Pro users in the U.S. on web and iOS. OpenAI says users can connect financial accounts through Plaid, see a dashboard, ask questions about spending and goals, and disconnect accounts later.

The product is built around context. Instead of asking a generic budgeting question, a user can ask about recent spending, subscriptions, upcoming payments, goals, or scenarios while ChatGPT uses linked account data.

OpenAI also says the experience cannot make changes to accounts. That matters: the current shape is analysis and planning, not bill payment, trading, or moving money.

## Why It Matters

Personal finance is one of the first AI categories where convenience and risk arrive together. The same context that makes advice useful is also sensitive: income, debt, purchases, investments, and life plans.

The right use case is decision support. Ask it to find patterns, prepare questions for a professional, compare scenarios, or summarize tradeoffs. Do not treat it as a replacement for tax, legal, investment, or debt advice.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="20 min" prerequisites={["ChatGPT Pro in the U.S., or a normal finance app", "One money question you already track"]}>

1. Start without connecting accounts. Ask a normal planning question, such as "Help me reduce dining spend by $200 next month without making a strict budget."
2. If you have access to the preview, read the connection screen carefully before linking anything. Confirm what data is shared, how to disconnect, and where account controls live.
3. Ask review-only questions first: "What changed in my spending categories?" or "Which subscriptions should I inspect?"
4. Use the answer to make a checklist outside ChatGPT. Keep final decisions in your bank, brokerage, tax software, or with a qualified professional.
5. After testing, disconnect the account if you do not expect to use the feature regularly.

</PracticalGuide>
