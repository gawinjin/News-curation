## TL;DR

Axios reports that Anthropic passed OpenAI in Ramp's paid workplace AI adoption data for April 2026. Treat that as a useful signal, not a final market verdict. Teams should compare AI tools on the workflows they actually run.

## What's New

According to Axios, Ramp's dataset showed Anthropic ahead of OpenAI among U.S. businesses with paid AI model subscriptions in April. The reported gap was narrow, and the dataset reflects businesses visible through Ramp's expense platform.

That makes the finding useful but bounded. It does not mean Anthropic is bigger overall, better for every team, or guaranteed to keep the lead. It does suggest that Claude's workplace positioning is landing with many business buyers.

Stanford's 2026 AI Index gives helpful background: AI adoption is broadening quickly, but capability, safety, education, and organizational readiness are moving at different speeds.

## Why It Matters

The practical mistake is choosing an AI tool because a chart says one company is winning. A tool that is excellent for long documents may not be the best for spreadsheet automation, coding, customer support, or daily research.

The adoption race matters because it tells us where vendors are investing. But your team still needs a small, repeatable evaluation before standardizing.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="20 min" prerequisites={["A team using at least one AI assistant", "One repeated workflow to compare"]}>

1. Pick one real workflow, such as summarizing customer calls, drafting sales follow-ups, reviewing contracts, or writing internal reports.
2. Create a tiny test set: three realistic inputs and one example of a good output.
3. Run the same task in two AI tools with the same instructions.
4. Score the results on accuracy, usefulness, time saved, citation quality, and how much human cleanup was needed.
5. Choose the tool for that workflow only. Repeat the test before expanding to another department.

</PracticalGuide>
