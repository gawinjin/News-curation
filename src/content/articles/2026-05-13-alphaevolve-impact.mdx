## TL;DR

Google DeepMind says AlphaEvolve has moved from algorithm-discovery demos into real optimization work: genomics, power grids, quantum circuits, TPUs, databases, logistics, advertising, and chemistry. The common pattern is not magic. It is a measurable problem where candidate solutions can be tested.

## What's New

DeepMind's update says AlphaEvolve has been used to improve DNA sequencing accuracy, grid optimization, natural-disaster prediction, quantum-circuit design, cache policies, TPU design, Spanner compaction, logistics routing, and model training speed.

Some examples are deeply technical, but the practical shape is understandable: define a search space, generate possible solutions, run them against a score, keep the better ones, and repeat. That is why an "agent" can help in places where normal chatbots feel vague.

The public examples gallery also matters because it makes the process easier to inspect. You can see how a problem is represented and what it means for an algorithm to improve.

## Why It Matters

For most people, the lesson is not "use AlphaEvolve tomorrow." It is to notice which problems around you are measurable enough for AI-assisted search.

If you can score a solution, you can improve it. That might be a delivery route, a meeting schedule, a classroom seating chart, a packing list, a spreadsheet formula, or a test suite. AI agents become more useful when the task has constraints and feedback, not just a vague request to be clever.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="15 min" prerequisites={["One process you can measure", "A rough list of constraints"]}>

1. Pick a small optimization problem from your life or work: route order, shift schedule, budget allocation, or study plan.
2. Write down the constraints: time, cost, fairness, required stops, deadlines, or hard exclusions.
3. Define one score. Examples: total minutes, missed deadlines, cost, number of conflicts, or error rate.
4. Ask an AI assistant for three candidate solutions and a table comparing each candidate against the score.
5. Keep the scoring rule and improve it. The better your feedback signal, the more useful the agent becomes.

</PracticalGuide>
