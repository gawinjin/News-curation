## TL;DR

Google's biggest I/O model announcement is Gemini 3.5 Flash: a fast model aimed at agentic work, coding, Search, and the Gemini app. The practical test is simple: give it a multi-step task and see whether it keeps context while acting quickly.

## What's New

Google introduced Gemini 3.5 as its latest model family and started with 3.5 Flash. Google says Flash is available now in the Gemini app, AI Mode in Search, Google Antigravity, the Gemini API, Google AI Studio, Android Studio, Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, and Gemini Enterprise.

The positioning is clear: Flash is no longer just the cheap, quick model. Google is pitching it as a model for agentic workflows, coding, multimodal work, and long tasks that require planning across steps.

Google also previewed Gemini Spark, a personal agent built on 3.5 Flash, with trusted testing starting now and a beta planned for Google AI Ultra subscribers in the U.S. next week.

## Why It Matters

Most people experience model upgrades through small moments: a better first draft, a faster answer, fewer confused follow-ups. Gemini 3.5 Flash matters because Google is putting one model into many daily surfaces at once: Search, Gemini, developer tools, and enterprise products.

That means the same model behavior may start showing up when you ask a web question, build a small app, summarize a document, or use a Google-backed workplace tool. If it is reliable, the benefit is convenience. If it is wrong, the risk spreads just as widely.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="10 min" prerequisites={["A Google account", "Access to Gemini or Google Search AI Mode"]}>

1. Open Gemini and ask for a plan for one real task, such as comparing three insurance options, planning a weekend trip, or organizing a messy note.
2. Add one constraint after the first answer, such as a budget, deadline, or accessibility need.
3. Ask Gemini to turn the plan into a checklist with owners, dates, and open questions.
4. Check whether it preserved your original constraints or quietly dropped one.
5. Save the prompt if it worked. Reuse it later to compare Gemini 3.5 Flash against another assistant.
