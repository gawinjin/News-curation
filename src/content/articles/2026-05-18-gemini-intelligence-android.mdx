## TL;DR

Google's Gemini Intelligence preview is bigger than another assistant button. It points to Android becoming a place where AI can act across apps: fill forms, summarize pages, turn rough speech into polished text, and create widgets from plain language.

## What's New

Google says Gemini Intelligence will roll out in waves, starting with newer Samsung Galaxy and Google Pixel phones this summer, then expanding to other Android devices, watches, cars, glasses, and laptops later in the year.

The most important feature is multi-step app automation. Google describes tasks like finding information in Gmail, building carts from lists, handling rides or bookings, and tracking progress while the user gives final confirmation.

Google is also adding Gemini in Chrome for mobile research and comparison, smarter Autofill that can use connected app context, Rambler for cleaner voice-to-text messages, and natural-language widget creation.

## Why It Matters

Phones are full of small chores: copy this into that, fill a form, summarize a page, book a thing, rewrite a message, check a detail. If AI can do those tasks reliably, the phone becomes less like a grid of apps and more like a task surface.

The risk is control. Any assistant that moves across apps needs clear permission boundaries, visible progress, and final confirmation before expensive or irreversible actions.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="15 min" prerequisites={["An Android phone", "One repetitive phone task"]}>

1. Write down one phone task you repeat often, such as copying grocery lists, filling appointment forms, rewriting messages, or comparing products.
2. Break it into steps and mark the step where you must approve the result.
3. When Gemini Intelligence reaches your device, test only that one workflow first.
4. Watch for three things: whether it used the right source data, whether it showed progress clearly, and whether it stopped for confirmation.
5. Keep using manual app controls for payments, purchases, bookings, or messages until the approval flow feels predictable.
