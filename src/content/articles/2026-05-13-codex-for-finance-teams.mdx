## TL;DR

OpenAI published practical Codex examples for finance teams: cleaning models, refreshing reporting packs, building variance bridges, and comparing scenarios. The best use is not "make me a forecast." It is "check the workbook, cite the cells, flag assumptions, and draft owner questions."

## What's New

The finance guide shows Codex being used around familiar finance workflows: operating plans, CFO and board packs, variance analysis, forecast refreshes, and scenario planning. The common thread is source-backed work. Codex is asked to review models, tie numbers back to inputs, and separate confirmed drivers from questions.

That is a better pattern than asking an AI to invent business judgment. Finance work depends on trust, traceability, and human ownership. A useful agent should speed cleanup, reconciliation, and explanation while making unsupported numbers louder.

The guide also points to a broader shift: AI work is moving from chat prompts into files, slides, spreadsheets, emails, and team channels. The assistant becomes more useful when it can inspect the actual materials.

## Why It Matters

Spreadsheets are full of invisible risk: hardcodes, stale links, broken formulas, sign errors, mismatched periods, and numbers that no longer tie to a source. Those are exactly the kinds of issues a patient assistant can help surface.

The human still owns the decision. But an AI agent can make the review packet cleaner: what changed, where the numbers came from, what is unsupported, and who needs to answer which question.

<PracticalGuide timeToTry="15 min" prerequisites={["A spreadsheet or reporting pack you are allowed to analyze", "A finance owner who can review assumptions"]}>

1. Choose one low-risk file: a copy of a forecast, KPI tracker, or monthly reporting pack.
2. Ask the assistant to inspect structure first: tabs, formulas, hardcodes, broken links, sign conventions, and obvious checks.
3. Require a memo, not just edits. It should list fixes made, unsupported numbers, assumptions, and exact tabs or cells needing review.
4. For a variance bridge, ask it to separate confirmed drivers from questions for owners.
5. Have a human finance owner review every business assumption before sharing the result.

</PracticalGuide>
